Easy Steps to Filter Emails from Specific Senders in Gmail

Filtering emails from specific senders in Gmail is a simple process that can help you easily manage your inbox and prioritize important messages. By setting up sender filters, you can ensure that emails from specific individuals or domains are automatically organized into a designated folder, making it easier for you to stay on top of your important communications.

To begin, log in to your Gmail account and navigate to the Settings menu. Locate the "Filters and Blocked Addresses" tab and click on it. From here, you can create a new filter by clicking on the "Create a new filter" button. In the pop-up window, you have the option to specify the criteria for the filter, such as the sender's email address or domain. Once you've entered the desired criteria, click on the "Create filter" button to proceed. Next, you'll need to decide what action you want Gmail to perform when an email meets the specified criteria. You can choose to automatically archive it, mark it as read, or even forward it to a different email address. After selecting the desired action, click on the "Create filter" button, and voila! You have successfully set up a filter for emails from a specific sender.
