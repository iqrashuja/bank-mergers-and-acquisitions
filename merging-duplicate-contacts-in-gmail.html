Merging Contacts: Streamline Your Gmail Address Book

When it comes to managing contacts in Gmail, having duplicate entries can be a hassle. It not only clutters your address book but also makes it difficult to find the right contact when you need to. Luckily, Gmail offers a convenient feature that allows you to merge duplicate contacts, streamlining your address book and making it easier to stay organized.

To merge duplicate contacts in Gmail, simply navigate to your Contacts page. From there, select the contacts you wish to merge by clicking the checkbox next to each entry. Once you have selected the contacts, click on the "More" button at the top of the page and choose "Merge Contacts" from the dropdown menu. Gmail will then merge all the selected contacts into a single entry, combining their information and eliminating any duplicates. This process not only saves you time but also ensures that your contacts' details are up to date and easily accessible.
